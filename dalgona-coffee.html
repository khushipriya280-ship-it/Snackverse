Tips for Creamy Texture

  • Must Use Instant Coffee: Ground coffee beans or espresso will not whip up. Instant coffee undergoes a drying process that leaves starch-like residues which act as stabilizers for foaming.
  • Boiling Hot Water: Make sure your water is boiling hot. Hot water dissolves the sugar and coffee instantly, allowing the bubbles to trap air and stiffen faster.
  • Keep Ratios Identical: Always stick to the 1:1:1 ratio. The sugar is a structural binder; reducing it will make the coffee foam airy, runny, and prone to deflating quickly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use decaf coffee?

Yes, decaf instant coffee whips up just as thick and creamy as regular instant coffee! Just make sure it is indeed instant coffee granules.

Can I make this with less sugar or a sugar substitute?

Sugar is critical to keeping the whipped coffee stable. While you can reduce sugar slightly or use stevia, the foam will deflate much faster and won't hold its shape as well.

Can I use warm or hot milk?

Absolutely! Dalgona coffee is traditionally served iced, but spooning the whipped coffee foam over warm steamed milk makes a delicious hot beverage. The foam will dissolve slightly faster on hot milk.

What types of alternative milk work best?

Oat milk and soy milk work best for alternative milks because they are naturally thicker and creamier, providing a great taste balance to the bitter whipped coffee.
