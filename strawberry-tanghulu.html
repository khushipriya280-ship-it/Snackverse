Strawberry Tanghulu

Tips for Beginners

  • Must Be 100% Dry: Any lingering water on the strawberry skin will create a barrier, causing the caramel to slide off or dissolve into a sticky syrup mess.
  • Do Not Stir Boiling Sugar: Stirring will agitate sugar crystals, causing the mixture to crystallize into a grainy, opaque paste instead of a clear glass shell.
  • Coat as Thinly as Possible: A quick single roll in the sugar syrup is enough. A thick sugar shell is extremely hard to bite and can hurt your teeth!

Safety Tips for Handling Sugar Syrup

  • Sugar Burns are Severe: Boiling sugar syrup reaches 150°C (300°F). It behaves like hot wax, sticking to the skin and causing deep, painful burns. Keep children and pets clear of the stove area.
  • Keep Cold Water Nearby: Always have your bowl of ice water ready before starting. If hot sugar contacts your skin, immediately submerge the area in the ice water to stop the burn.
  • Use Long Skewers: Ensure your wooden or bamboo skewers are long enough to keep your hands and fingers well clear of the hot boiling pan when dipping.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did my sugar shell melt or get sticky?

Tanghulu absorbs moisture quickly. Sticky tanghulu is caused by wet fruit, high humidity in the room, or if the syrup didn't heat long enough to reach the hard-crack stage (150°C / 300°F).

Can I use other types of fruit?

Yes! Seedless grapes, mandarin orange segments, kiwi slices, blueberries, and cherry tomatoes all make fantastic tanghulu. Make sure they are washed and dried completely.

How long does Tanghulu last? Can I store it in the fridge?

Tanghulu is best eaten within 1–2 hours. Do not refrigerate long-term; the moisture in the refrigerator will cause the sugar to liquify, resulting in a sticky, melted mess.
