Tips for Beginners

  • Use Block Feta: Avoid using pre-crumbled feta. Crumbled feta contains starch additives to prevent clumping, which prevents it from melting into a smooth, creamy sauce.
  • Save Your Pasta Water: Don't dump all the pasta water! The starchy pasta water is the secret to getting a glossy, cohesive sauce that binds to the noodles.
  • Quality Olive Oil: The olive oil forms the body of your sauce. Use a good extra virgin olive oil rather than regular vegetable oil for the best flavor profiles.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use low-fat feta cheese?

It is highly recommended to use block, full-fat feta cheese. Low-fat feta has a higher water content, doesn't melt as creamy, and can become rubbery or watery when baked.

What kind of pasta works best?

Short, textured pasta shapes like Cavatappi, Penne, Rigatoni, or Rotini are best because the ridges and hollow spaces hold onto the creamy tomato-feta sauce beautifully.

Can I add protein to this dish?

Absolutely! Grilled chicken, sautéed shrimp, or chickpeas make fantastic additions. Simply toss them in at the end when mixing the pasta and sauce together.

Can I make this gluten-free?

Yes, simply substitute the regular pasta with your favorite gluten-free pasta (chickpea or brown rice pasta work well). Be careful not to overcook gluten-free pasta as it breaks easily.
