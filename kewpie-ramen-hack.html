Tips for Creamy Broth

  • Whisk Constantly While Tempering: Slowly adding hot water while whisking rapidly is the key. The egg yolk and mayo emulsify with the starchy water to create a velvety broth rather than curdling.
  • Under-cook Noodles Slightly: Cook the noodles for 30 seconds less than the package instructions. They will absorb broth and soften slightly in the bowl as you eat.
  • Kewpie Mayonnaise is Required: Western mayonnaise is made with whole eggs and vinegar, which curdles and tastes sour. Kewpie is made with only egg yolks and apple cider vinegar, giving it its signature rich flavor.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is it safe to eat the raw egg in this recipe?

Yes! Tempering the raw egg by slowly whisking in the boiling hot ramen water cooks/pasteurizes it safely, creating a smooth creamy broth instead of scrambling it.

Can I use regular mayonnaise?

You can, but it will not taste the same. Western mayonnaise contains more vinegar and whole eggs, which might curdle or make the broth taste sour. Japanese Kewpie mayo uses only egg yolks and monosodium glutamate (MSG) for maximum umami richness.

What flavor of instant ramen works best?

Spicy instant ramen (like Shin Ramyun) or savory soy/miso-based ramens work best. The fat from the egg yolk and Kewpie mayonnaise cuts through the hot spice, smoothing out the heat and balancing the broth beautifully.
